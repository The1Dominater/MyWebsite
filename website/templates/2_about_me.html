My Journey in Computer Science👨‍💻

When I started my degree in Computer Science at Regis University, all I really wanted was to make Minecraft mods and only went to school based on my parents advice. After getting past the introductory courses, I my interested was peaked while taking classes like Computational Theory, Operating Systems, and Paradigms of Programming languages.

Realizing Regis's Computer Science department was limited by its size, I transferred to CU Boudler to gain access to a greater variety of courses and often with greater depth to them. At CU I took introductory courses in quatum computing, robotics, cyber-security, and bio-networking. However, the classes I enjoyed the most, system administration and data center computing, pretty much because (Super Computers == cool) evaluates to True.

During my senior year at CU Boulder, I worked with FESTO and senior project team to develop a software based system for generating unique ids for a specific device based on irrepicable defects in the device's memory. The idea was to pull initialize binary values from the device's memory on start up and pplug them into a Physcially Unclonable Function(PUF) in order to generate a unique id only which could only be generated by the given device. Then it would send the id to a web based Solidity contract where a business or organization could grant the device access to their service(s) or system(s). I learned a lot about planning and building, and then re-planning when everything went wrong🔥📉🤔.

Most recently, I interned with the National Renewable Energy Labratory through the SULI program. I worked within the Power Systems Engineering Center to help develop a LLM for analyzing power system question a utility company may face and assist the utility operators in addressing the problem. My mentor, Rishab Jain, and I focused on developing an multi-agent AI system where each AI agent was an expert in a specific domain, such as system maintance, cost analysis, and human comfort. The expert agents then would bounce ideas off each other in an attempt to build a more robust and thought out solution.
